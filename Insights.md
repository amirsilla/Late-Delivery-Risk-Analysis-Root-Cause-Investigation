# Late Delivery Risk Analysis & Root Cause Investigation

## Business Problem

The objective of this project is to identify the factors contributing to high late delivery risk and evaluate its impact on business performance.

## Dataset Information

* Dataset: DataCo Supply Chain Dataset
* Records Analyzed: 180,519
* Tools: Python, Pandas, Jupyter Notebook, Matplotlib

## Key Metrics

* Total Sales: 36.78 Million
* Late Delivery Risk: 54.83%
* Revenue Associated with Late Deliveries: 20.13 Million
* Revenue Associated with On-Time Deliveries: 16.66 Million

## Key Findings

### Shipping Mode Analysis

* First Class shipping has the highest late delivery risk at 95.32%.
* Second Class shipping has a late delivery risk of 76.63%.
* Standard Class has the lowest late delivery risk at 38.07%.

### Category Analysis

* Golf Bags & Carts has the highest late delivery risk at 68.85%.
* Lacrosse and Pet Supplies also show elevated delivery risk levels.
* Several product categories exceed the overall average delivery risk.

### Regional Analysis

* Central Africa has the highest late delivery risk.
* South Asia, East Africa, and Western Europe also experience significant delivery challenges.
* Delivery performance varies across regions, indicating operational differences.

### Financial Impact

* More than 20 million in sales are associated with delayed deliveries.
* Delayed orders account for a larger share of revenue than on-time orders.
* Delivery performance issues may negatively affect customer satisfaction and retention.

## Business Recommendations

1. Investigate First Class shipping operations and delivery partners.
2. Improve logistics planning in high-risk regions such as Central Africa.
3. Monitor product categories with consistently high delivery risk.
4. Implement delivery performance tracking dashboards.
5. Optimize shipping routes and warehouse allocation to reduce delays.

## Conclusion

The analysis revealed that shipping mode, product category, and region significantly influence delivery performance. By addressing these operational bottlenecks, the organization can reduce delivery delays, improve customer satisfaction, and protect revenue associated with high-value orders.
